In the rush to address immediate challenges, "Ready, aim, fire!" gets flipped to "Fire, ready, aim!"

Organisational leaders face constant pressure to act quickly when addressing customer demands and operational problems. But reversing the traditional decision-making sequence — acting before fully assessing situations — creates hidden risks that compound over time.

What appears to be swift, decisive action often carries costs that only surface later: rework, misaligned resources, and solutions that treat symptoms rather than causes.

Despite time constraints, pausing for strategic thinking produces superior long-term outcomes compared to immediate reactive responses. The discipline to aim before firing — to diagnose before prescribing — is what separates sustainable improvement from firefighting.

The question every operations leader should ask: are we solving problems, or are we creating new ones faster than we solve the old?
