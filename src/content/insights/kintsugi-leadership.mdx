In Japan, *kintsugi* is the art of repairing broken pottery with lacquer mixed with gold. The repair doesn't hide the break — it illuminates it. The object becomes more beautiful, and more valuable, because of its history.

Leadership works the same way.

## The Myth of the Unbroken Leader

We celebrate leaders who appear invincible — who never stumble, never doubt, never fail. But this myth creates organisations that hide their fractures instead of learning from them.

When a leader treats failure as something to conceal, the entire organisation learns to do the same. Defects get buried. Near-misses go unreported. The culture of continuous improvement dies quietly.

## Leading with Gold

Kintsugi leadership means:

- **Acknowledging the break** — publicly, without shame
- **Investing in the repair** — not a patch, but a genuine strengthening
- **Making the repair visible** — so others learn that breaking is not the end

The strongest quality cultures are built by leaders who have failed, learned, and made their learning visible. They don't pretend the system was never broken. They show you the gold.
